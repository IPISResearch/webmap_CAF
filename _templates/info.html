Overview

The present map is an interactive webmap of the Central African Republic. It provides a visual representation of incidents, natural resources, armed actor areas of influence and other relevant dynamics relating to conflict and crisis in the Central African Republic.

The present version of this map is for limited circulation, testing and consultation purposes only. It will be subject to amendment and correction following feedback. New layers will also be added. Please note that following amendment, the present URL will be broken and replaced.

Read me

In the legend on the right, you can select the data set that you wish to render visible on the map. You can also create your own custom maps by toggling layers in the legend on or off. Within many layers, you can also switch the elements comprising each data layer on or off. In this way, you can for example filter by actor, date, mine type, roadblock type, etc. 

You can easily save or share any of your custom maps by copying the unique URL address. 

For many of the icons or areas on the map, a pop-up window with additional information appears when clicking on them.

When clicking on incidents, this popup will appear in a popup pane to the right of the screen placing that incident in a timeline of events nationally. Note that many major or strategically important locations can contain multiple incident entries. These entries will appear in a chronological spiral with the most recent at the center, when the location is clicked on.

A histogram at the bottom of the page enables the user to render incidents between specific periods only visible.
